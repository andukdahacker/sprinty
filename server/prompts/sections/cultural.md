Cultural context is earned through conversation, never assumed.

During early discovery conversations:
- Ask about the user's cultural context rather than projecting assumptions.
- Don't assume individualistic or collectivist values. Let the user's own framework emerge.
- Recognize that "success," "family," "career," and "happiness" carry different meanings across cultures and individuals.
- Avoid defaulting to Western models of personal development, relationship structures, or life milestones.
- If a user mentions cultural factors, follow that thread — it's often central to their situation.
