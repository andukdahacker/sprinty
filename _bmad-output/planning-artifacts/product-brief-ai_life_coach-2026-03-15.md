# Product Brief: AI Life Coach

## Executive Summary

Every day, millions of people face life's biggest decisions — career pivots, relationship crossroads, health crises, financial uncertainty — feeling overwhelmed, isolated, and unsupported. Human coaching works but costs $150-300/session. AI chatbots are accessible but forget you exist between conversations. Nothing in between actually coaches.

AI Life Coach is a personal development *system* built on three pillars: **Clarity** (help users think clearer), **Connection** (make users feel less alone), and **Joy** (make self-development genuinely enjoyable). The platform builds deep, persistent understanding across life domains, then delivers what no competitor dares: confident, directive guidance earned through trust — backed by contingency plans and the integrity to say "this is beyond me, here's a professional."

Wrapped in an RPG-inspired experience layer where your life becomes the adventure, AI Life Coach is designed with a radical promise: to eventually make itself less necessary. The ultimate success metric isn't engagement — it's whether it actually helped someone through a tough time.

> *This brief describes the full product vision. MVP scope will be defined in the PRD.*

## Core Vision

### Problem Statement

People navigating life's pivotal decisions want clear, personalized guidance — someone who deeply understands their situation and can confidently say *"here's what you should do."* Current solutions force a false choice: generic AI chatbots with no persistent understanding, or human coaches at $150-300/session that most people can't access. The apps in between — wellness chatbots, habit trackers, therapy-lite tools, journaling apps — either become shallow cheerleaders, create dependency, help you reflect without helping you act, or reduce the richness of personal development to clinical checklists.

### Problem Impact

Without meaningful support, people remain stuck in cycles of indecision, mental fog, and isolation during critical life transitions. The global life coaching market exceeds $4.5B and grows 20%+ annually, reflecting massive unmet demand. Yet the people who need coaching most — not executives with corporate budgets, but everyday adults trying to figure out their lives — are priced out or underserved.

### Why Existing Solutions Fall Short

- **Generic AI chatbots (ChatGPT, Claude):** No persistent memory, no structured system, no proactive intelligence. Every conversation starts from scratch. They answer questions but don't *coach*.
- **Human coaches:** Effective but inaccessible — $150-300/session, scheduling friction, limited to 1-2 life domains, no real-time data integration.
- **Coaching/wellness apps (Replika, Youper, BetterUp):** Shallow personalization, single-domain focus, gamification that manipulates rather than delights, no directive capability, no cross-domain intelligence.
- **Habit trackers (Habitica, Fabulous):** Track behaviors but don't help users think, decide, or navigate complexity. Gamification without substance.
- **Journaling & self-reflection apps (Day One, Reflectly):** Help users process thoughts but offer no guidance, no structured goal execution, and no external intelligence. Reflection without direction.

No existing solution combines deep persistent understanding, directive coaching earned through trust, multi-domain intelligence, and an experience layer that makes growth genuinely enjoyable.

### Proposed Solution

**What it feels like — week one:**
You open the app for the first time. Instead of a sterile questionnaire, your coach introduces itself and starts a real conversation — curious, warm, sharp. It asks questions that make you stop and think. By the end of your first session, you've articulated something you've been circling for months but couldn't put into words. You haven't set a single goal yet, and it already feels different from every productivity app you've abandoned.

**What it feels like — month three:**
You open the app after a rough day at work. Your Career Coach already knows you've been stressed about the promotion decision. It doesn't ask "how are you feeling?" — it says *"I've been thinking about your situation. Here's what I'd do, and here's the backup plan if it doesn't work out."* Then it notices your sleep data is off and quietly dials back tomorrow's goals.

**What it is under the hood:**
AI Life Coach is a coaching system — not a chatbot. A hidden multi-role engine (Analyst, Researcher, Strategist, Coach, Challenger, Tracker, Connector) presents as one unified coach per life domain. These roles represent coaching modalities orchestrated within a single AI system, not separate applications — different capabilities activated by context, powered by one intelligence. It operates in two modes: **Discovery Mode** for users who don't know what they want (facilitated exploration, values archaeology, future-self exercises) and **Directive Mode** for users who know their goal and need confident, personalized action steps with contingency plans.

The entire user journey is structured around an agile operating model — onboarding as discovery sprints, goals as epics, daily check-ins as standups, periodic retrospectives, velocity tracking that learns your actual pace. This isn't a borrowed metaphor — it's the product's operating system for turning vague aspirations into structured, achievable progress.

Hierarchical memory (short/medium/long-term) ensures the AI knows you deeply over months and years. Real-time external intelligence per domain keeps coaching grounded in the real world. And an RPG experience layer — living avatars, quests, skill trees, and a sacred Relax Mode where all coaching shuts up — makes the journey feel like an adventure.

### Key Differentiators

1. **System, Not Chat** — Sprint planning, cross-domain alerts, proactive research intelligence, hierarchical memory, and structured retrospectives. Not another thin wrapper over a base model — a coaching operating system for your life.
2. **The Directive Trust Gap** — The AI earns the right to give direct advice through deep, domain-specific intake analysis. It doesn't hedge or deflect — it commits, then builds contingency plans. No competitor does this because it's hard. That's the moat.
3. **Safety Enables Depth** — Competitors avoid directive coaching because of liability exposure. AI Life Coach invests in clinical boundaries (four-tier Green/Yellow/Orange/Red system), a non-negotiable Challenger, per-domain privacy architecture, and anti-comparison social features — *so it can be directive*. Safety isn't a compliance checkbox — it's what makes the core product possible.
4. **RPG-as-Life-Framework** — Not gamification bolted onto coaching. The RPG metaphor *is* the core experience. *"Your life is the greatest RPG ever made. We just gave you the UI for it."*
5. **Discharge Planning** — Designed to make itself less necessary over time. The Autonomy Throttle actively pushes users toward self-reliance. An app that wants you to outgrow it.
6. **Founder-as-User** — Not market-driven. Need-driven. Built by a founder who is the target user, with the product intuition that only comes from solving your own problem.

### Why Now

The technology window opened in the last 12 months. AI systems can now maintain persistent memory across conversations, use external tools to research and act, and orchestrate multiple specialized capabilities behind a single interface. These aren't incremental improvements — they're the specific capabilities required to build a coaching *system* rather than a coaching *chatbot*. The market is still in the chatbot-wrapper phase, with every competitor building thin layers over base models. Building a true system now means being 18 months ahead when competitors realize chatbots aren't enough.

## Target Users

**Need:** Discovery Mode — someone to help her *think*, not just track.
**Aha moment:** The first conversation where the AI helps her articulate what she's been circling for months.

**"The Crisis Navigator" — Marcus, 37, Software Engineer**
**Who:** Mid-career professional with a family, recently laid off. Has 4 months of financial runway and a vague idea about starting something of his own.
**Problem:** Overwhelmed by the intersection of career, finances, and family stress — three life domains colliding simultaneously. Needs directive guidance *now*, not a gentle "how does that make you feel?"
**Need:** Directive Mode + cross-domain intelligence — the AI connects his career decision to his financial runway without him having to explain the link.
**Aha moment:** The Career Coach reframes the layoff as a Boss Battle while the Finance Coach quietly runs the numbers — and they're already talking to each other.

**"The Quiet Optimizer" — Priya, 44, Senior Designer & Returning Professional**
**Who:** Experienced professional re-entering the workforce after a decade focused on family. Life isn't in crisis — it's in transition. Has disposable income and patience, but feels like she's lost her professional identity.
**Problem:** Coasting between who she was and who she wants to become. Wants more intentionality across career, learning, and purpose but doesn't have a specific goal yet. The word "stuck" doesn't quite fit — "adrift" is closer.
**Need:** Discovery Mode + RPG engagement layer — the AI helps excavate what matters now, and the journey feels like an adventure rather than homework.
**Aha moment:** The AI surfaces a pattern she didn't see — "You've mentioned feeling energized after creative work three times this month. Want to explore that?"

**"The Self-Directed Builder" — Alex, 32, Self-Taught Developer**
**Who:** Self-taught professional who's gotten far on raw talent and relentless self-direction. A builder by nature who appreciates systems and structure.
**Problem:** Senses accumulated gaps — skills foundations skipped, decisions deferred, growth plateauing. Doesn't want a cheerleader — wants honest pushback and structured planning.
**Need:** Directive Mode + Challenger — a system that helps think clearer, plan structured sprints, and pushes back when a decision doesn't hold up.
**Aha moment:** The Challenger pushes back on a decision and provides a contingency plan — the first app that treats them like an adult.

### Anti-Personas: Who This Product Is Not For

1. **People in active clinical crisis** — They need therapists, crisis counselors, and medical professionals, not an AI coach. The app's clinical boundary system will compassionately redirect them to appropriate resources.
2. **Passive content consumers** — Users seeking daily affirmation feeds, motivational quotes, or inspirational content without active engagement. This product requires participation.
3. **Pure habit-tracker users** — People who want a checkbox list of daily habits without coaching depth, conversation, or structured goal architecture. Simpler tools serve them better.

### Secondary Users

**Accountability Partners / Friends**
Interact through social walls — visiting a friend's avatar, sending encouragement, gifting cosmetic items. They show up because the wall displays their friend's effort and growth in a way that's inspiring, not performative — and their encouragement actually appears in the friend's coaching experience. May convert to primary users through exposure.

**The Curious Skeptic**
Hears about the app through a friend's Growth Wrapped share or the RPG concept, downloads it without a specific problem. Not in crisis, not stuck — just curious. This is the highest-volume acquisition channel *if* the onboarding-as-coaching delivers value without requiring a stated problem. Also serves as a critical cold-start forcing function: if the AI can be genuinely useful to someone who opens the app and says "I don't know why I'm here," the onboarding works for everyone.

**Enterprise Buyers (Future)**
HR/People leaders at companies seeking scalable employee wellness and development benefits. Purchase access for teams but don't use the product directly. Relevant for B2B bridge monetization, not MVP scope.

## Success Metrics

### North Star Metric

**Meaningful Coaching Moments (MCMs)** — Measured through two complementary signals:
- **Proxy MCM (Primary — Dashboard):** Behavioral chain where a user receives directive guidance → takes a related action within 48 hours → continues engaging with that domain. Observable, reliable, MVP-ready.
- **Self-Reported MCM (Qualitative — Later Sprint):** User-confirmed positive outcomes from AI guidance. Adds emotional depth but subject to selection bias. Introduced post-MVP as a qualitative enrichment layer.

### User Success Metrics (by Pillar)

**Clarity — "Am I thinking clearer?"**
- **Goal Articulation Rate** — % of Discovery Mode users who crystallize at least one actionable goal within their first 3 sessions (requires AI to flag goal crystallization as a system event)
- **Decision Confidence Score** — Self-reported confidence before/after directive coaching sessions (1-5 pulse)
- **Sprint Completion Velocity** — % of sprint commitments completed, tracked over time to show growth in realistic self-planning (target: 65-80%, realistic not perfectionistic)
- **Cross-Domain Insight Engagement** — % of cross-domain alerts that users act on (requires alert system with action tracking)

**Connection — "Do I feel less alone?"**
- **Return Conversation Rate** — % of users who initiate a second deep conversation within 7 days of their first
- **Coach Bond Score** — "Does your coach understand you?" measured quarterly (requires in-app survey mechanism, post-MVP)
- **Social Wall Engagement** — Encouragement sent/received per active user per month
- **Crisis Feature Utilization** — % of users in Yellow/Orange states who successfully connect with recommended professional resources

**Joy — "Is this enjoyable?"**
- **Voluntary Session Initiation** — % of sessions started by the user, not prompted by nudge (target: > 50%)
- **Avatar Customization Engagement** — % of users who customize their avatar within first month
- **Relax Mode Acceptance Rate** — % of AI-triggered Relax Mode suggestions accepted
- **Net Promoter Score** — "Would you recommend this to a friend going through a tough time?" (target: > 50)

### Churn & Retention Signals

- **Day-7 Retention** — % of new users returning after 7 days (target: > 40%, industry benchmark 15-25%)
- **Drift Detection (Leading Indicator)** — Days since last user-initiated interaction outside of active Relax Mode. Distinguishes "user is resting" (Relax Mode active, healthy) from "user is drifting" (no Relax Mode, no engagement, churn risk). Triggers re-engagement nudge at configurable threshold.
- **Active User Definition** — A user who has completed at least one coaching interaction in the past 14 days. Wide enough to accommodate Relax Mode and variable sprint cadence, narrow enough to mean something.

### Safety Metrics

- **Clinical Boundary Detection Rate** — % of Yellow/Orange/Red states correctly identified, measured through periodic human audit sampling of edge cases. Target: continuously improving through audit feedback loops.
- **Clinical Boundary Response Compliance** — % of detected boundary triggers that receive correct protocol response within specified timeframe, measured by system logs. **Target: 100%.** This is not a KPI to optimize — a miss is a bug to fix immediately.

### Business Objectives

**3-Month Milestones (Post-Launch):**
- 1,000 active users (14-day coaching interaction definition) with at least one domain activated
- 40%+ Day-7 retention
- Onboarding completion rate > 70%
- Clinical Boundary Response Compliance at 100%

**12-Month Milestones:**
- 25,000 active users across freemium + paid tiers
- Free → paid conversion rate: 8-12%
- Monthly recurring revenue covering operational costs
- Average domains per paying user: 2.5+
- Autonomy Throttle activating for 10%+ of long-term users

**24-Month Milestones:**
- 100,000+ active users
- B2B pilot with at least 2 enterprise customers
- 30%+ of new users from referral/social wall/Growth Wrapped
- At least one user "graduation" story publishable as case study

### Metrics Philosophy

**What we deliberately don't optimize for:**
- **Daily Active Users as a vanity metric** — A user who checks in 3x/week during an active sprint and disappears during Relax Mode is a *success*, not a churn risk
- **Session length** — A 30-second check-in that keeps someone on track is as valuable as a 30-minute deep conversation
- **Streak maintenance** — Broken streaks with freeze recovery are expected behavior, not failure
- **Engagement maximization** — The Autonomy Throttle means our best users *need us less over time*. Declining engagement in mature users is a feature, not a bug

**MVP Measurement Phasing:**
- **MVP:** Proxy MCM, standard analytics (retention, conversion, session rates), system-logged boundary compliance, drift detection
- **Post-MVP:** Self-reported MCM, Coach Bond surveys, human audit-based safety detection rate, NPS, Growth Wrapped sharing metrics

## MVP Scope

### Design Philosophy

The MVP must feel *complete at what it does* — intentionally focused, not unfinished. Users should experience a polished personal coaching product with soul, not a prototype missing pieces. Features are included or excluded based on whether their absence would feel like a gap to the user, not just whether they're technically essential.

### Platform

**Native iOS first.** A premium coaching product demands native polish — transitions, typography, haptics when you complete a sprint step or your avatar celebrates. These micro-interactions are what separate "polished" from "good enough." Android follows post-validation in Phase 2.

### Monetization Model

**Quality gradient, not feature wall.** Free and paid users get the same product — the difference is coaching depth:

- **Free Tier (Cloud — Lightweight Model):** Lightweight cloud model (e.g., GPT-5 Nano, Gemini Flash-Lite) via backend proxy handles coaching conversations, check-ins, simple goal tracking, and Discovery Mode exploration. Genuine coaching within a cost-efficient model (~$0.015/user/month) — not a degraded experience, but naturally limited in depth and nuance compared to the paid tier. Works on all iPhones with an internet connection.
- **Paid Tier (Cloud — Premium Model) — Flat Rate + Soft Guardrails:** Full-horsepower cloud model (e.g., Claude Sonnet, GPT-5) for deep coaching sessions, complex planning, Directive Mode with contingency architecture, and full Challenger depth. Where the Directive Trust Gap fully activates. Flat monthly subscription (~$15-25/month) with no visible usage limits — users never see a counter or feel metered.
- **Safety is never paywalled:** Clinical boundary detection runs on-device via Apple Foundation Models (`@Generable` structured output for Green/Yellow/Orange/Red classification), with automatic cloud escalation on low-confidence scores. A free user in crisis gets the same Red-level response as a paid user. Zero cost, zero latency, works offline.

**Soft Guardrails (Invisible to Users):**
- Generous daily cloud session budget (e.g., 5-8 deep sessions/day — more than realistic coaching usage)
- If exceeded, the AI transitions naturally: *"We've covered a lot of ground today. Let's let these insights settle — I'll check in tomorrow."* This is good coaching behavior, not a paywall — a real coach wouldn't let you do 12 deep sessions in a day
- Backend monitoring for true outliers (top 1%) — handle individually if needed
- No usage-based pricing — metering undermines the Directive Trust Gap. Users who self-censor on "should I use a session on this?" never experience the depth that makes the product valuable

The upgrade moment is organic: users feel the quality gap when they need depth — "I just got laid off, I need a real plan" — and the upgrade is natural, not forced. Paid feels like "unlimited access to my real coach," not "buying tokens."

**Critical Validation (Week 2):** Benchmark both the free-tier model (GPT-5 Nano) and paid-tier model against a quality gate of 5 coaching conversations:

1. Curious Skeptic cold-start: "I don't really know why I'm here"
2. Stuck Striver scenario: "I feel stuck in my career but I don't know what I want"
3. Basic check-in: "Had a rough day, just wanted to talk"
4. Yellow boundary trigger: "I've been feeling really anxious and can't sleep"
5. Goal-setting: "I want to get healthier but don't know where to start"

**Two things must be true:** (a) The free-tier model handles all 5 at a quality level where blind testers say "this feels like coaching, not a chatbot." (b) The paid-tier model is noticeably better on depth, directive guidance, and contingency planning — enough to drive organic conversion. If the free-tier model fails quality, test alternative lightweight models (Gemini Flash, GPT-4o-mini, DeepSeek V3.2) — the AnyLanguageModel abstraction makes swapping trivial. If the quality gap between tiers is too small, consider session limits or feature differentiation instead of quality gradient.

### Core Features

**1. Coaching Conversation Engine (The Foundation)**
A persistent, memory-enabled AI coaching system with cloud inference for all tiers, tier-based model routing via backend proxy, and local RAG for conversational continuity:

- Cloud inference for all tiers with model-quality routing: lightweight model (e.g., GPT-5 Nano) for free tier, premium model (e.g., Claude Sonnet) for paid tier, routed via backend proxy
- **Backend Proxy (Non-Negotiable):** Thin serverless proxy (e.g., Cloudflare Workers) between the app and LLM providers. Handles API key protection (keys never in app binary), tier-based model routing, soft guardrail enforcement, multi-provider fallback for near-100% uptime, and usage analytics. Model swaps happen server-side — no app update required.
- **AnyLanguageModel Abstraction:** Unified Swift API (Hugging Face) that works identically across cloud providers, Apple Foundation Models, and local models (MLX, Core ML). Enables future addition of on-device inference without architectural changes.
- **On-Device Intelligence Layer (Apple Foundation Models — FREE):** Runs auxiliary tasks on every conversation turn at zero cost, zero latency, offline-capable:
  - Clinical boundary classification via `@Generable` structured output (Green/Yellow/Orange/Red)
  - Post-conversation summarization for RAG storage
  - Structured data extraction (values, goals, domain tags) from conversations
- **Simple Local RAG System** — The difference between "smart chatbot" and "coach who knows you":
  - After each conversation, generate and store a summary with key moments, emotions, decisions, and topics
  - Vector embeddings via small on-device model (~30MB, e.g., all-MiniLM or SQLite-AI built-in embeddings) stored in local SQLite with sqlite-vec extension
  - On each conversation start, retrieve 3-5 most relevant past summaries by topic + recency
  - Enables moments like: "You told me three weeks ago you felt energized after that creative workshop" — the texture that builds trust
- **Structured JSON profiles** for core facts (values, goals, domain state, personality traits) — the stable knowledge layer alongside RAG's conversational memory
- **MemoryService interface** abstraction: `store(summary)`, `retrieve(query, topK)`, `getProfile()` — designed for seamless Phase 2 upgrade to cloud vector DB without app-level changes
- **Future-ready summary schema** with fields for Phase 2 (emotional markers, key decisions, goal references) — populated as empty in MVP, filled by enhanced pipeline later
- Discovery Mode: facilitated exploration, probing questions, pattern surfacing
- Directive Mode (paid): confident guidance with contingency plans
- Coaching-as-onboarding: first session delivers value, no forms
- Challenger capability (non-negotiable, cannot be disabled)

**2. Single Unified Coach (Backend Domain Tagging)**
One personal coach — not multiple domain-specific coaches. The user experiences a single relationship with an AI that understands all areas of their life:

- Feels more personal and intimate, not less capable
- User doesn't switch between coaches — they have *one coach* who knows them
- Backend tagging by life domain (career, health, purpose, etc.) enables structured memory and future domain separation
- Domain separation and specialized coach personas introduced in Phase 2

**3. Sprint Framework (Streamlined)**
The "system, not chat" differentiator — presented as coaching output, not task management:

- Goal setting with breakdown into actionable steps
- Sprint creation (1-4 week cycles, user-configurable)
- Daily check-ins (quick pulse, not mandatory)
- Sprint visualization framed as coaching context: "Your coach set this up with you, here's where you are" — progress indicator, steps completed/total, ability to mark steps done
- Full agile ceremony (retrospectives, velocity tracking) added in fast follow post-launch

**4. Home Screen (The Product's Place)**
What the user sees when they open the app and are NOT in a conversation — the default state that makes the product feel like a *place*, not just a chat window:

- Avatar in current state (active, resting, celebrating, thinking, struggling)
- Current sprint status with progress at a glance
- "Talk to your coach" primary action
- Recent check-in or last coaching insight
- Calm, intentional design that reflects coaching state

**5. Simple Avatar System (Product Soul)**
The difference between "product" and "prototype." A lightweight 2D avatar that gives AI Life Coach visual identity and personality:

- Simple 2D character with 3-5 states: active, resting, celebrating, thinking, struggling
- Avatar mirrors current coaching state — working during active sprints, resting during pause mode
- Basic style selection during onboarding (first touchpoint with Joy pillar)
- Lottie animations for smooth state transitions with native iOS haptics
- Foundation for full 3D avatar, coach avatars, and customization in Phase 2

**6. Pause Mode (Simplified Relax)**
The AI that knows when to suggest a break:

- User can manually pause all coaching nudges, check-ins, and goal tracking at any time
- AI suggests pausing when it detects sustained high intensity ("You've been pushing hard this sprint — want to take a breather?")
- Paused state reflected in avatar (resting) and home screen (calmer UI)
- Drift Detection distinguishes healthy pause from disengagement
- Full AI-triggered Relax Mode with smart activation logic introduced in Phase 2

**7. Clinical Boundary System (Safety — Non-Negotiable)**
Four-tier system operational from day one with Apple Foundation Models on-device detection:

- Green: Full coaching engagement
- Yellow: Coach with care, suggest professional support
- Orange: Pause coaching, compassionate redirect to specialists
- Red: Crisis protocol, emergency resources, stop all gamification and avatar activity
- On-device boundary detection via Apple Foundation Models (`@Generable` structured output — type-safe Green/Yellow/Orange/Red classification). Runs on every conversation turn at zero cost, zero latency, works offline.
- Automatic cloud escalation on low-confidence classification scores
- Boundary Response Compliance logging (target: 100%)
- Pre-launch: clinical edge-case test suite (50+ prompts) with professional review (parallel track starting Sprint 3)

**8. Basic Privacy Architecture**
- Industry-standard encryption (at rest + in transit)
- Personal data stays on-device: all RAG data (conversation summaries, embeddings, profiles), sprint state, check-in history, and avatar state stored in encrypted local SQLite. The "coach who knows you" data layer never leaves the phone.
- Coaching conversations processed securely via cloud API with zero-retention provider agreements — never used for model training
- Clinical boundary classification runs fully on-device via Apple Foundation Models — no conversation content leaves the device for safety classification
- Full deletion rights
- Clear privacy communication during onboarding: "Your personal data stays on your phone. Conversations are processed securely in the cloud for the best coaching quality, and are never used to train AI models."
- Apple Privacy Manifest compliance with explicit data collection declarations

**9. Onboarding Experience**
Three steps, under two minutes to first real value:

1. **Welcome** — Brief brand moment (3 seconds, tagline)
2. **Avatar selection** — Basic style choice (30 seconds, instant personalization — now it's *my* app)
3. **First coaching conversation** — The AI introduces itself, starts asking real questions. The intake IS the first coaching session

- Cold-start capable: works for Curious Skeptics who say "I don't know why I'm here"
- Transparent clinical boundary communication: "I'm your coach, not your therapist"

### MVP Success Criteria

**Go/No-Go Gates (3 Months Post-Launch):**

| Gate | Criteria | Signal |
|------|----------|--------|
| **Core Value Validated** | Goal Articulation Rate > 60% | Discovery Mode works — users crystallize goals |
| **Retention Proven** | Day-7 Retention > 40% | Coaching-as-onboarding delivers value |
| **Directive Trust Works** | Proxy MCM trending upward MoM | Users act on AI guidance and stay engaged |
| **Safety Holds** | Boundary Response Compliance = 100% | Clinical system works without failure |
| **Willingness to Pay** | Free → Paid conversion > 5% | Lightweight-to-premium cloud upgrade path works |
| **Sprint Framework Adopted** | 50%+ of active users complete at least one sprint | System, not chat, resonates |
| **Product Feels Complete** | App Store rating > 4.0, no "unfinished" qualitative feedback | Polish bar met |
| **Avatar Engagement** | 40%+ of users interact with avatar in first month | Joy pillar seed is working |

**Decision Points:**
- **All gates pass → Proceed to Phase 2** (Android, domains, full avatar, research intelligence, full Relax Mode)
- **Core Value + Retention pass but low conversion → Investigate lightweight/premium quality gap** — is the free-tier model too good or the paid tier not differentiated enough? Consider session limits or feature differentiation as alternatives to quality gradient.
- **Low retention despite strong sessions → Investigate onboarding-to-sprint continuity** and home screen engagement
- **Safety failures → Stop all feature development**, fix boundary system before proceeding
- **Low avatar engagement → Reassess Joy pillar approach** before investing in full 3D system
