## Phase 1: First Principles — The Bedrock

Five foundational principles that define what makes this product philosophically distinct from any coaching app on the market:

### Principle 1: The Directive Trust Gap

Users want to be told what to do. Not because they're lazy — because they trust an entity that deeply understands their situation. The AI earns the right to be directive through depth of analysis. This flips the "we don't give advice" coaching orthodoxy — personalized directive advice, earned through deep understanding, is what people actually crave.

### Principle 2: Domain-Specific Depth Profiles

Trust isn't built through a single generic questionnaire — it's built by demonstrating deep understanding within the specific life domain the user needs help with. Each domain (career, relationships, health, finances) has its own "knowledge prerequisites" before directive advice becomes credible. A separate, deep intake analysis per domain.

### Principle 3: Dual-Mode Operation

Two fundamental modes:

- **Discovery Mode** — "I don't know what I want" → Facilitated exploration using structured discovery techniques (brainstorming methods, provocative questions, future-self exercises, values archaeology) to excavate what the user actually wants
- **Directive Mode** — "I know what I want, tell me how" → Confident, personalized action steps

The AI is a discovery facilitator first, then transitions to a directive coach once goals crystallize.

### Principle 4: Respectful Challenge + Contingency Architecture

The AI earns the right to push back by never withdrawing support after the pushback. It flags risks, presents alternative perspectives, but when the user decides — the AI commits fully to that path AND proactively builds contingency plans. "If you're doing this, here's Plan B and Plan C if it goes south." The challenge and the safety net come as a package.

Example: *"I notice your goal to take that VP role conflicts with the work-life balance you ranked as your #1 value. Are you sure? → Yes → Then let's go get it. Here's the plan. And here's what we'll do if the work-life balance starts cracking."*

### Principle 5: Living Knowledge, Not Static Advice

The AI is a bridge between deep self-knowledge AND real-time world knowledge. It actively researches, monitors, and surfaces external intelligence relevant to the user's goals. A career agent tracking job market shifts, salary trends, emerging skills demand, company layoffs and hiring sprees — proactively bringing that to the user.

Each domain agent has its own intelligence feed:

- Career Agent → industry trends, job postings, skill demand, company news
- Health Agent → nutrition research, fitness methods, health alerts
- Finance Agent → market conditions, interest rates, investment opportunities
- Relationship Agent → psychology research, local events, workshops

### Analogy 1: Agile Software Development → Personal Development

| Agile Concept | Life Coach Equivalent |
|--------------|----------------------|
| Sprint | Focused growth period (1-4 weeks) |

| Standup | Daily check-in |
| Retrospective | Periodic life review |
| Technical Debt | Skipped foundations that create growth ceilings (e.g., self-taught dev needing CS fundamentals) |
| Definition of Done | Progressive mastery levels: Awareness → Understanding → Application → Teaching → Integration |
| CI/CD | Continuous integration of small daily habits into larger goals |
| Code Review | Structured life decision review against stated values and goals |
| Pair Programming | Structured accountability partners with driver/navigator roles |
| Feature Flags | Gradual life changes — controlled rollout instead of drastic shifts |
| Velocity Tracking | Personal growth velocity — AI learns actual pace and plans realistically |

**Life Technical Debt** is a major concept — shortcuts, skipped foundations, and deferred development that worked to get you here but won't get you there. The AI actively identifies and surfaces debt across domains as strategic investment opportunities.

**Definition of Done — Progressive Mastery Levels (Bloom's Taxonomy mapped):**

1. Familiar — I've seen it, I know what it is
2. Understand tradeoffs — I know pros and cons
3. Can go deeper — I know enough to research independently
4. Applied — I've used it in real work
5. Can teach it — I can explain it to someone else
6. Systems thinking — I see how it connects to the full picture

## Phase 5: Reverse Brainstorming — Guardrails & Ethics

### The 9 Critical Guardrails

| # | Guardrail | Protects Against | Implementation |
|---|-----------|-----------------|----------------|
| 1 | **Autonomy Throttle** | Learned helplessness / dependency | AI monitors decision dependency, actively pushes users toward self-reliance. Reduces directive frequency as confidence grows. Discharge planning. |
| 2 | **Sacred Relax Mode** | Toxic optimization / burnout | When active, ALL coaching shuts up. AI proactively triggers based on sprint intensity, milestones, time, stress signals. |
| 3 | **Positive-Sum Gamification** | Shame and guilt spirals | Stats plateau but never visibly decrease. Streaks have freezes. Missed goals = respawn/checkpoint, never failure. Social wall shows effort, never rankings. |
| 4 | **Hard Clinical Boundaries** | Playing doctor/therapist | Four-tier system: Green (coaching), Yellow (sensitive — suggest professionals), Orange (referral — pause coaching), Red (crisis — emergency resources, stop all gamification). |
| 5 | **Privacy as Architecture** | Data weaponization | Encryption, per-domain privacy levels, data minimization, full deletion rights, audit trails, legal framework for subpoenas. |
| 6 | **Non-Negotiable Challenger** | Echo chamber reinforcement | Cannot be turned off. AI willing to lose engagement to protect user wellbeing. |
| 7 | **Anti-Comparison Social** | Inadequacy and toxic competition | No global leaderboards. Walls show effort, not speed. Encouragement only. Full privacy option always available. |
| 8 | **Cultural Adaptability** | Western-centric bias | Onboarding understands cultural context. Hybrid domain model accommodates any life structure. AI asks, never assumes, what "a good life" means. |
| 9 | **Zero Tolerance Content Safety** | Sexual content, predatory behavior, grooming | Content moderation on all social touchpoints. Avatar moderation. One-tap reporting. Instant ban on confirmed violations. Pattern detection for grooming. Vulnerability-aware protections. Trust & safety team from day one. |

### Clinical Boundary System (Detail)

| Level | What's Happening | App Response |
|-------|-----------------|-------------|
| Green | Goal setting, motivation, accountability | Full coaching engagement |
| Yellow | Stress, mild anxiety, general sadness | Coach with care, suggest professional support as option |
| Orange | Persistent mood issues, trauma, disordered eating, substance concerns | Pause coaching, compassionate redirect to specialists |
| Red | Suicidal ideation, self-harm, abuse, immediate danger | Crisis protocol, emergency resources, stop all gamification |

**Extends beyond mental health:**

- Physical health symptoms → doctor
- Complex financial/legal situations → advisor/lawyer
- Domestic abuse → counselor/support services

**Transparency from onboarding:** *"I'm your life coach, not your therapist. If you're dealing with clinical challenges, I'll connect you with real professionals. That's not a limitation, that's me looking out for you."*

### Origin Story

A friend sees a change on their wall and sends encouragement. Their avatar doesn't crumble — it puts on armor.
